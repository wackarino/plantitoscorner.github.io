Five best plants for the beginners

This five plants are the best if you are a beginner. These kind of plants are also for indoor only so put it inside your home, make sure that it will not have direct sunlight and must be put in pots.If you still haven’t tried your hand in gardening this quarantine, this is a sign that you should finally take the plunge. There is a reason why so many people have transformed into plantitos and plantitas over quarantine; and while many are still intimidated by the concept of becoming plant parents, we put together a list of indoor plants that will surely change your mind!

POTHOS

If you’re eyeing those vine-y or trailing plants, you’re in luck! The Pothos is another indoor plant that is quite easy to take care of. While it starts off pretty small, you may need to repot this plant just because it grows quite quickly.

You can place your Pothos in almost any part of your room as it can survive in moderate indoor light, and low light conditions.

Also when you notice that the leaves are looking pale,it means too much sun, and loss of color means too little.




ALOE VERA

Before you buy an aloe, note that you’ll need a location that offers bright, indirect sunlight. However, the plant doesn’t appreciate sustained direct sunlight, as this tends to dry out the plant too much and turn its leaves yellow, rendering them subpar for use.

Keep them watering down to a minimum! Aloe Vera plants like their soil pretty dry before they go through another round of watering.

If you love Aloe Vera for its healing properties, then you'll love it even more after we tell you it’s incredibly easy to take care of.




SNAKE PLANT

Snake Plants are pretty low-maintenance, which is most probably why many starting plant mom and dads get this as their first plant baby. This type of plant is known to survive droughts, so if you miss a couple of watering days, we promise it won’t die on you.

Although this type of plant would thrive more under direct sunlight, it can actually survive in lower light conditions. Snake plants also make for good air-purifying plants as it's been shown to filter chemicals like benzene, xylene and formaldehyde to name a few.




SPIDER PLANT

The Spider Plant isn’t a fan of direct sunlight, so you’re going to want to place this a little farther from the window.

This vivacious number is sure to turn your space into the urban jungle you’re dreaming of, just as long as it is watered at least once a week and its receiving bright indirect sunlight.

Spider plants needs to grow in a soil-based, well-draining potting mix. Spider plants like even moisture, they don't like to be too dry or too wet.




ZZ PLANT

The ZZ Plant thrives in indirect sunlight. If you’ve got space across from your window, this plant will grow happily in its little corner.
Like the Snake Plant, the ZZ Plant is pretty drought tolerant, with some even saying they water it only once a month.

ZZ plant is also has a benefit against insects and diseases. It is very resistant to diseases and insects.
If your plant's branches intrude into your space and get in the way, simply prune them away
