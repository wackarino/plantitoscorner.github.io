Taking care of plants

1.) Choose the pots

Make certain there are one or more holes in the bottom of your container to allow water to flow
out freely.Insufficient drainage can cause roots to drown, and the plant to die prematurely.

2.) Choose the potting mix

Do not use soil from the yard or garden. It can be filled with weed seeds, insects, and fungal diseases.buy potting soil
because it loose and is a light mixture of materials like peat moss, vermiculite and often,decomposed organic matters.

3.) Choose the plants

"Putting the right plant in the right place" because spacewise don't try to grow a flower like a rose in which
requires six hourse of full sun inside your house or on a porch that gets only an hour in the early morning.

Always use one kind of plant per pot and if you use to combine multiple types of plants, make sure they all
like the same light and moisture conditions.

4.) Prepare the pots

If your containers are large, place them where they’ll ultimately go before filling them.Once they are full and
watered, they may be too heavy to move.Put a basket type filter or a shard of broken pot to the holes in the bottom
to prevent the potting mix from washing out but will still allow water to escape

Before putting the soil, check the moisture content if its too humid or too wet. generally you need to add water
a little at a time and knead the mixture with your hands.A good rule of thumb is to wet the mixture until it
feels like a damp sponge.

5.) Pot the plants

Remove the plants from its nursery container. and make sure to be atleast an hour before transplanting.
this will ease their removal and diminish transplant shock. Support the top of the "root ball" by placing a
finger on each side of the stem then tip the pot and let the plant fall gently into your hand. and never pull
out by its stem. and tap the pot to loosen it.

WATERING

if you planted in the spring and the weather is mild you can water the plants for like once a week. and in summer
plants need more water. not only is the warm weather evaporating the moisture before the plant can use it, the plants
need more water as they grow larger. hanging the plants and small pots may need watering twice a day(morning and evening)
and for the large pots once a day is enough.

water your plants until the water comes out of the drainage holes. that way you know the soil is getting moisture to the bottom.

water the soil and not the leaves and water and if the plants and flowers look wilted in the hottest time of the day as
long as the top of the soil is most you don't need to water and Don't let pots sit in water, this can cause roots to drown.

FEEDING

Plants growing in containers need more fertilizing than those in the ground. The more you water, the more quickly you flush
the nutrients out of the soil. It’s good to use a time-release fertilizer when planting (see “Step 2: Choose the Potting Mix”),
but it’s the bare minimum. If you want really healthy and happy plants, feed them a liquid or water-soluble fertilizer every
couple of weeks according to package directions.

DEHEADING

Pinching or cutting off faded blooms, known as deadheading, is essential. It encourages a plant to keep producing more flowers.
Some plants have so many tiny flowers and stems, it would be too time-consuming to snip or pick off individual flower heads.
For those types, it’s best to shear the whole plant back to about one-third of its size. It will look “whacked” for about a
week, but you will soon be rewarded with a flush of new buds and blooms.
